Overview

Simple and Exact Dependence

This lesson illustrates how PolyAnalyst can be used for performing an automated discovery of a formula that relates two attributes. The data consists of two attributes (variables): x and y. Pairs of values of these attributes were generated artificially according to a simple formula. The objective of the study is to determine the explicit form of this formula by analyzing the two columns of data.


Medical Study

The user learns to manipulate datasets, create various graphs for data visualization, and create and apply rules in the PolyAnalyst environment. The data for this lesson were taken from the archive of a large heart surgery clinic. Each of 109 records corresponds to one patient. The follow information about each patient is stored: file number (ID), sex, age, diagnose, weight, and height.


Miles Per Gallon Analysis

We find an empirical rule that relates the MPG (miles per gallon) characteristic of a car with other technical parameters of the car. The data consists of 398 records characterizing various car types. For each car type the following attributes are provided: the MPG value (mpg), which was measured for each car model in a test performed in 1982, the number of the engine cylinders (cyl), a cylinder displacement in cubic inches (displ), the engine power (power), the car weight in lb.(weight), a number of seconds required to accelerate to the speed of 100 miles per hour (accel), the car production year (year), the country of production (origin: USA, Europe, or Japan), and the name of the model (model).


Miles Per Gallon Prediction

This tutorial is an extension of Tutorial 3b and is directed more toward using data from the previous analysis to help in predicting miles per gallon.


Forcasting the Direction of Gold Price Change

We embark on predicting the direction of change of gold price in the COMEX trading system between the two sequential sessions. The data consists of the values of various stock market indexes, such as Standard & Poor, and the indexes, characterizing the performance of individual industry groups. The data covers 1,077 trading sessions, or about a three-year period.


Applied Marketing Research

To better understand the lifestyles of potential purchasers of the Land Rover Discovery, marketing research manager commissioned a study of consumers' attitudes, interests, and opinions. A questionnaire was designed with 30 statements covering a variety of dimensions, including consumers' attitudes towards risk, foreign vs. domestic products, product styling, spending habits, self-image, and family.


Direct Marketing

In this lesson, PolyAnalyst is used to predict buyers of a service based on easily available data about a set of businesses. This tutorial is based on an actual data analysis done by Megaputer for a telecommunications company, though the numbers and data fields have been changed and the amount of data reduced and simplified to speed the analysis. The client was introducing a new product, and would thus have to call prospective customers or send out mass-mailings to companies that would be interested in a new telecommunications service. This is an expensive proposition, and the company turned to data mining to find a way to reduce the cost of direct marketing while increasing the payoff.


Market Basket Analysis

In this tutorial, we will examine a manufacturer of electronic appliances and components. Our example company has over 250 products, each identified by a 3-character code. The goal of this exploration is to find out which of these products sell well together, so that when a customer or distributor purchases some products, the manufacturerŇs sales representatives can offer related products with an increased chance of making a sale.


PolyAnalyst Scheduler

In this lesson, we will learn how to implement the PolyAnalyst Scheduler, which is a tool that provides batch process data mining in Polyanalyst. We will be using the same data that is used in tutorial 3 to create a simple batch processing example. The goal of this tutorial is to get you familiar with the Scheduler.
